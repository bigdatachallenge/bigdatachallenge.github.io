What is the Big Data Challenge?

Now in its fourth year, the STEM Fellowship Big Data Challenge is a competition that helps high school students acquire data science skills by investigating solutions to the United Nationâ€™s 16 development goals. During the competition, students will analyze a dataset of their choice and will express their findings in a scholarly report. The winners of the preliminary round will attend Big Data Day at SAS Canada and pitch their analysis towards a panel of industry experts.

Timeline

  • Team Registration

    October 20th, 2017

    This deadline has passed! You can find the recording of the orientation session here

  • Project Development Begins

    October 21st, 2017

    Teams are paired with mentors, chosen specifically for their expertise in the industry. Mentors work to guide and support students through their project. Teams are provided with data science workshops designed by STEM Fellowship, along with mentorship in Overleaf, an online, collaborative platform to design project reports with basic coding. Additional tools and resources which students can benefit include the following SAS resources:
    Getting Started with Graphics in SAS Univeristy Edition
    Free SAS University Edition Tutorials

  • Full Project Report Submission

    January 19th, 2018

    Deadline for Full Project Report submission, that is, the full Overleaf research paper with all its findings must be submitted by midnight.

  • Reviewers Review Submissions

    Weeks of January 22nd, 2018 & January 29th, 2018

    Reviewers revise project submissions and produce the top 6 finalists for the competition.

  • Announcement of Finalists

    Week of February 5, 2018

    Finalists then prepare presentations to deliver before a panel of judges on Big Data Day.

  • Big Data Day

    February 22, 2018

    Finalist teams will present in front of a judging panel. There will also be a Roundtable discussion on Future of Data Science and Analytics Careers with industry experts, as well as an award ceremony where the top 3 teams are recognized
